About Cancer Support

The word “survivor” means different things to different people. The National Coalition for Cancer Survivorship defines a cancer survivor as someone who lives with, through and beyond cancer. Other definitions focus on the time from diagnosis through the rest of one’s life. Whether you use the word “survivor,” or prefer a word like “warrior,” the idea is the same. Being a survivor means taking an active, positive role in your own care. There are more than 14 million cancer survivors in the United States. This number continues to grow as better ways to diagnose and treat cancer are discovered. This group has specific medical, physical and emotional health needs - needs that, according to a 2006 report by the Institute of Medicine, are often not met.
